Here are some tips that you need to know before you start to bake!

Measure Properly

This is one of the most important rules in baking. Use the spoon and level method for dry ingredients: spoon the ingredients into a dry measuring cup and level the top with a straight edge. Do not pack it in, you’ll end up with way too much that you should. The only ingredient that you should ever pack into a cup is brown sugar. Liquid ingredients are more straightforward, always measure them in a clear measuring cup on a stable and flat surface.

Use Room Temperature Ingredients

It’s important to follow through when a recipe asks for room temperature ingredients. It’s best to leave ingredients overnight, but there are ways to get around this: For eggs, one could place them in a bowl and run them under warm tap water for a few minutes. For butter, one could microwave it.

Don’t Make Substitutions

There really aren’t many good substitutes for all-purpose flour, sugar, butter or eggs. Experiment at your own risk.

Don’t Overmix

Mixing causes the result to have a chewy texture, which is something that we don’t want in cakes and muffins. When a recipe says so, make sure to not overmix and just go until it is uniform.
